About Eric - The Founder


He has a 15 years career in the telecommunications and information technology industry and has held various roles in research and development, strategy, innovation, marketing, product development. He has practiced in both European, African and Middle Eastern markets.
After graduating as an engineer in Telecommunications and Multimedia in 2001 at the Ecole Nationale Supérieure des Ingénieurs in Caen, France, Eric Ntonfo began his career with EservGlobal group in the Paris region, which provided services for operators in Africa and Asia South East. He was then in charge of operational support on the VAS (Value Added Services) and IN (Intelligent Networks) platforms of Vodacom in Tanzania and Hutchinson in India, two mobile operators in the emerging market.

In 2002, he joined the Research & Development Division of then France Telecom, the French giant incumbent operator. He lead innovation activities on the seamless mobility of applications on the Internet between heterogeneous wireless access (GSM/GPRS and Wifi). For four years, He was a delegate for France Télécom at the Internet Engineering Task Force (IETF), the body that specifies communication protocols for the Internet. He participated in working groups on mobility in the Internet. He also represented France Telecom at the Institute of Electrical and Electronic Engineers (IEEE) 802 wireless forum

In 2005, he was named Leader of the partnership between France Telecom and Motorola on fixed/mobile convergence. He led the realization on a 3G / Wifi Motorola mobile device prototype, which showcased a proof of concept of transfer of a Voice over IP call from a 3G to a Wifi access without breaking of the call.

In 2007, France Telecom was renamed Orange. Eric took at that time responsibility within Orange Labs for the management of a unit of 20 people responsible for conducting optimization studies on the 3G network for Orange France, and for defining the contours of the 4th generation of mobile telephony. He launched the European project ARTIST4G which brought together mobile operators and equipment manufacturers to define the bases of LTE Advanced, technology at the heart of future developments in 4G networks.

In 2011, he joined the Africa and Middle East division of Orange where he was responsible for driving innovation for Central Africa (Cameroon, DRC, Niger) and Botswana. He launched along with the local teams many value-added services such as emergency credit, advanced voicemail, Orange wireless access, and Orange Money.

In 2013, he also became an entrepreneur, developping and later launching two digital services in finance technology: www.kwendoo.com, an application to better link scattered communities together and foster efficient solidarity to improve life of those back home and www.fiatope.com a platform to drive the growth of local initiatives in Africa by connecting original projects to new funding sources.

In 2015, pursuing his career at Orange, he became Project Director within Orange France Product Marketing, in charge of deploying innovation that should revolutionize user experience of digital authentication and authorization by using a new simple and strong SIM card-based technology: Mobile Connect.

Eric Ntonfo also has a dozen years of experience as a lecturer in engineering and management schools in France and Cameroon in as many areas as Internet technologies and wireless networks, digital services in emerging markets and entrepreneurship in the digital era.
